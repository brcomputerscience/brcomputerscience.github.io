About Us

The Bridgewater-Raritan Computer Science Club (BRCS) is a student-led club that focuses on computer science and programming topics. We meet after-school on Tuesdays and offer opportunities to enhance students' computer science knowledge. We have lessons and opportunities in various fields, from Python to Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, catering to all skill levels. Whether you're a novice or an experienced coder, there's something for everyone. Join us to socialize, learn, and collaborate with like-minded individuals passionate about computer science.

Meetings

The Bridgewater-Raritan Computer Science Club hosts meetings after school every Tuesday, from 2:30 to 3:45 in room E-2. We have over 50 members working on projects and lessons in groups, each equipped with leaders who have demonstrated proficiency in what they're teaching through hackathons, programming competitions, and projects. Our hackathon-winning instructors teach members how to write code, think critically, and work together cohesively. Our groups range from Python and Java all the way to Computer Engineering and C++. We also occasionally host tournaments, where members can show off their coding and computer science trivia knowledge.
